The reasons of falling/Rising in GDP of India during British period

India was by far the world’s largest producer of cotton textiles. Indian textiles had long been renowned both for their fine quality and exquisite craftsmanship. They were extensively traded in Southeast Asia (Java, Sumatra and Penang) and West and Central Asia.European trading companies began buying Indian textiles for sale in Europe.By the beginning of the nineteenth century english made cotton textiles successfully ousted Indian goods from their traditional market in Africa, America and Europe and this led to huge fall in Indian economical health.
